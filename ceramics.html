Ceramics

In the field of ceramics there are many different uses for vessals, materials and the like as well as manufacturing methods and practical uses for ceramic objects. Throughout history ceramic vessals have served a purposeful role in various civilizations and have taken on role as utility devices as well as filled various artistic and religious roles.

Uses for Ceramics Throughout Time

Today

In modern times, ceramics has helped to improve technologies and products derived from ceramic materials can be seen throughout the house and even on the street. Working with ceramic materials isn't hard, and doing so can create an interest in the ceramic arts and foster an affection towards the material and an appreciation of genius uses of a primative material.

In History

If you've ever been to a museum, perhaps you've seen ancient Sumerian cuneform tablets, letters and symbols written in clay to express some kind of meaning or record. Sculptures of idols, or figures have also been created using ceramic materials. Vessals for eating, or to serve ceremonial purposes such as funeral rites have been made of clay as well. The historical use cases for clay is vast and diverse.

How Ceramic Pieces Are Made Today

In terms of creating ceramic pieces there are a few popular methods that are taught in studios, schools and universities today. Those methods include hand-building techniques such as: coil built, slab built, reductive/additive methods, but there are other methods using tools such as molds and pottery wheels.

Steps to Creating A Ceramic Piece

  1. Preparing the clay to be used
  2. Wedging the clay
  3. Building the form
  4. Allowing the clay to dry
  5. Firing the clay (now known as greenware)
  6. Retrieving the fired greenware (now known as bisqueware)
  7. Optional: Glazing the bisqueware
  8. If glazed: Final firing of glazed bisqueware
